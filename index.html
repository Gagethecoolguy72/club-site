Safety first, always

Range Rules

Sixty years of safe operation comes from everyone following the same simple rules, every visit.

1. Muzzle discipline. Keep every firearm pointed downrange at all times.
2. Treat every gun as loaded. No exceptions, ever.
3. Finger off the trigger until your sights are on target and you're ready to fire.
4. Eyes and ears required for everyone on or near a firing line.
5. Obey all range commands. "Cease fire" means stop immediately, unload, and step back.
6. Cold range means hands off. No handling firearms while anyone is downrange.

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the club open to the public?
Black Creek is a private membership club. Non-members can shoot as the guest of a current member ($30/day, up to two guests per member, three-hour sessions) — a great way to check the club out before joining.
Does the club rent or provide firearms?
No — bring your own firearms and ammunition. The club does not provide rental guns.
Do I need to be an NRA member to join?
Yes. As an NRA-affiliated range, all members must hold a current NRA membership. Annual NRA memberships can be purchased online in a few minutes.
Is there a waiting list or initiation fee?
Neither. Once your application and safety briefing are complete, you're a member. Memberships run the calendar year.
How long can I stay per visit?
Members have no time restrictions — come as often as you like and stay as long as you want during open hours. Guests are limited to three-hour sessions.
Where exactly is the club?
4292 Range Rd, Mechanicsville, VA 23111 — in Hanover County near the intersection of I-295 and I-64, about 15 minutes from downtown Richmond.
